Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a referral to see a chiropractor?

No referral is required. You can book directly online at any time.

What can I expect during my first visit?

Your first visit includes a detailed health history, discussion of your goals, a physical examination, and movement assessment. If appropriate, treatment may begin on the same visit.

Will I receive treatment on the first appointment?

In most cases, if it is safe and appropriate, treatment may begin on the first visit.

What conditions do you commonly treat?

Common concerns include back pain, neck pain, sports-related injuries, joint pain, posture-related discomfort, and movement limitations.

Do you provide medical devices such as custom orthotics or compression stockings?

When clinically indicated, assessment and prescription of custom orthotics or compression stockings may be part of your care plan to support function and symptom management.

Custom Orthotics & Medical Devices

When clinically indicated, medical devices may be recommended as part of a comprehensive chiropractic care plan. These devices are prescribed based on assessment findings and individual functional needs.

Custom Foot Orthotics

Custom foot orthotics are prescribed following a biomechanical and functional assessment. They are designed to support foot mechanics and may be used as part of a broader plan addressing movement, posture, or load management.

Many extended health benefit plans provide coverage for custom orthotics when prescribed by a regulated health professional. Coverage varies by insurer and plan.

Compression Stockings

Compression stockings may be recommended when clinically appropriate to support circulation and lower-limb comfort. Selection is based on assessment findings and individual needs.

Some insurance plans include coverage for compression stockings when medical necessity criteria are met. Patients are encouraged to confirm coverage with their provider.

Insurance & Benefits Information

Coverage for medical devices such as custom orthotics or compression stockings depends on your individual benefits plan. Some insurers require documentation, including a prescription or biomechanical assessment.

Receipts and required clinical documentation can be provided to support reimbursement where applicable. Direct billing availability varies by provider.
